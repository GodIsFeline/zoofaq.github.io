Isn't a Dog's Brain like a Three Year-Old's?

It isn't uncommon to see claims that a dog's brain is equivalent in capacity to that of a two-to-three year old human.[0] A significant flaw with such a claim, especially in terms of sexual relations, is that their "three-year-old brain" is a measure of their intelligence, like their ability to count, remember words, or play tricks, not a measure of their sexual maturity. While an adult dog may not be able to count as high as a five year-old, they have a much deeper understanding (that is, an understanding at all) of the concept of sex and the pleasure it brings. For more on a dogs comprehension of deriving pleasure from sex, see Do Animals Find Sex Pleasurable? and Can Animals Consent to Sex?.
